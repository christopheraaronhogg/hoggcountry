## Baxter State Park (Katahdin) - CRITICAL

### Official Requirements

**Source:** [baxterstatepark.org/general-info/the-at/](https://baxterstatepark.org/general-info/the-at/)

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Permit Required** | Yes - Long-Distance Hiker Permit |
| **Cost** | Free |
| **Registration** | In-person at Katahdin Stream Ranger Station |
| **Advance Registration** | Not available - first-come, first-served |
| **Annual Limit** | 3,150 permits per calendar year |
| **Validity** | 7 days from issue date |

### Key Rules

1. **All AT hikers** (NOBO, SOBO, section, flip-flop) must secure permit before summit attempt
2. **Display permit** on pack, available for rangers on request
3. **The Birches campsite** - Only for NOBOs who hiked 100 continuous miles from Monson
4. **Getting picked up** during 100-Mile Wilderness disqualifies you from The Birches

### Timing Considerations

| Date | Status |
|------|--------|
| **Opens** | ~May 15 (weather dependent) |
| **Recommended deadline** | October 15 |
| **Closes** | October 15 for thru-hikers |
| **After Oct 15** | Summit attempts at your own risk, no ranger support |

### Weather Closures

- **Rangers WILL turn you around** in unsafe conditions
- Common late season (Sept-Oct)
- Wind, rain, lightning all trigger closures
- **Build 2-3 buffer days** into your schedule

### If Permit Limit Reached

If the annual 3,150 limit is reached (never happened as of 2025):
- Enter through Togue Pond Gate as day visitor
- The Birches campsite will be closed
- Same summit process, just different entry

### Contact

- **Phone:** (207) 723-5140
- **Website:** baxterstatepark.org

## Great Smoky Mountains National Park

### Official Requirements

**Source:** [nps.gov/grsm/planyourvisit/backcountry-camping.htm](https://www.nps.gov/grsm/planyourvisit/backcountry-camping.htm)

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Permit Required** | Yes |
| **Cost** | $40 (Thru-Hiker Permit) |
| **Registration** | Online at smokiespermits.nps.gov |
| **Validity** | 38 days (8 days/7 nights to traverse) |

### Thru-Hiker Definition (GSMNP)

You qualify as a thru-hiker if you:
- Begin your hike at least 50 miles outside the park
- End your hike at least 50 miles outside the park
- Only travel on the AT within the park

### Shelter Rules

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| **Shelter stays required** | Must stay in designated shelters (not tenting) |
| **One night per shelter** | Cannot stay multiple nights at same shelter |
| **Thru-hiker spaces** | 4 spaces reserved per shelter (Mar 15 - Jun 15) |
| **Overflow tenting** | Thru-hikers only may tent near shelter if full |
| **Regular backpackers** | Must have reservations, cannot tent near shelter |

### Mile Markers in GSMNP

| Location | Mile |
|----------|------|
| Fontana Dam (South entrance) | 165.9 |
| Davenport Gap (North entrance) | 237.4 |
| **Total AT in GSMNP** | ~71.5 miles |

### Key Shelters in GSMNP

All require shelter stay:
- Birch Spring Gap (170.4)
- Mollies Ridge (175.9)
- Russell Field (178.8)
- Spence Field (183.2)
- Derrick Knob (189.5)
- Silers Bald (195.0)
- Double Spring Gap (196.7)
- Mt. Collins (200.7)
- Icewater Spring (210.1)
- Pecks Corner (217.3)
- Tri-Corner Knob (222.2)
- Cosby Knob (229.9)

### Contact

- **Backcountry Office:** (865) 436-1297

## Shenandoah National Park

### Official Requirements

**Source:** [nps.gov/shen/planyourvisit/backcountry.htm](https://www.nps.gov/shen/planyourvisit/backcountry.htm)

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Permit Required** | Yes |
| **Cost** | Free |
| **Registration** | Self-registration at kiosks |
| **Advance Registration** | Not required |

### Rules

1. **Camp at least 0.5 miles from** paved roads, park boundary, or facilities
2. **Camp at least 25 yards from** streams and trails
3. **No camping within 10 yards** of No Camping signs
4. **Use huts/shelters** when available (first-come, first-served)
5. **Fires only in designated areas** (fire rings at shelters)

### Mile Markers in Shenandoah

| Location | Mile |
|----------|------|
| South entrance (Rockfish Gap) | ~870 |
| North entrance (Front Royal) | ~971 |
| **Total AT in SNP** | ~101 miles |

## AMC White Mountains (New Hampshire)

### Overview

The Appalachian Mountain Club operates 8 high-mountain huts in the White Mountains. While no permit is required, there are fees and policies to understand.

**Source:** [outdoors.org/thru-hiking-the-white-mountains/](https://www.outdoors.org/thru-hiking-the-white-mountains/)

### AMC Hut Options

| Option | Cost | Details |
|--------|------|---------|
| **Full Stay** | $150+ | Bunk + dinner + breakfast |
| **Work-for-Stay** | Free | 2 hours work, floor space + meal |
| **Thru-Hiker Pass** | $5-10/night | Campsites only |

### Work-for-Stay Program

| Detail | Policy |
|--------|--------|
| **Spots per night** | 2 per hut (4 at Lakes of the Clouds) |
| **Reservations** | Not available - first-come |
| **Arrival time** | 5-6 PM to be considered |
| **Work required** | 2 hours (may include morning tasks until 10 AM) |
| **Includes** | Floor space inside + hot meal |
| **Limit yourself to** | 1-2 work-for-stays total |

### Lakes of the Clouds "Dungeon"

| Detail | Policy |
|--------|--------|
| **Bunks** | 6 available |
| **Cost** | $10/night |
| **Reservations** | Not available |
| **First-come** | Yes |

### Thru-Hiker Pass

| Detail | Policy |
|--------|--------|
| **First night** | $10 (33% off) |
| **Subsequent nights** | $5 (66% off) |
| **Valid for** | 14 days |
| **Applies to** | Tentsite stays only |

### The 8 AMC Huts (NOBO Order)

| Hut | Mile | Elevation | Capacity |
|-----|------|-----------|----------|
| Lonesome Lake | 1813 | 2,764 ft | 46 |
| Greenleaf | 1822 | 5,291 ft | 48 |
| Galehead | 1829 | 3,800 ft | 38 |
| Zealand Falls | 1836 | 2,635 ft | 36 |
| Mizpah Spring | 1850 | 3,800 ft | 60 |
| **Lakes of the Clouds** | 1855 | 5,106 ft | 90 |
| Madison Spring | 1862 | 4,800 ft | 50 |
| Carter Notch | 1876 | 3,890 ft | 40 |

### Important Notes

1. **Have a backup plan** - Work-for-stay is never guaranteed
2. **Bring tent** - Stealth camping available below treeline
3. **Above treeline rules** - Must use designated areas only
4. **Weather danger** - Huts provide safety in storms

## Other Permit Considerations

### State Parks (Various)

Most state parks along the AT allow thru-hiker camping without reservation, but rules vary:

| State | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Georgia | No special permits |
| Virginia | Day-use fee at some areas |
| Maryland | No camping fees for AT hikers |
| Pennsylvania | No special permits |
| New Jersey | No special permits |
| New York | Bear Mountain area - designated sites only |
| Connecticut | No special permits |
| Massachusetts | No special permits |
| Vermont | Long Trail/AT shared - no permits |
| Maine | No special permits |

### Bear Canister Requirements

| Location | Required? |
|----------|----------|
| GSMNP | No (use bear cables at shelters) |
| Most of AT | No (use bear cables/boxes) |
| Mahoosucs (ME) | Recommended |

### Fire Restrictions

Fire regulations vary by:
- Season (often banned in dry periods)
- Location (above treeline = no fires)
- Shelter rules (use existing fire rings only)

**Best practice:** Use stove only, no campfires.
